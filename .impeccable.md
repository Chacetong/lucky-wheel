## Design Context

### Users
Social and general-purpose users who spin a lucky wheel at parties, gatherings, or whenever they need a fun random selection. The context is casual and communal — often with a group watching the screen together, anticipating the result. The app should feel immediately usable with zero learning curve.

### Brand Personality
**Fun, vibrant, energetic.** The interface should feel like a game — something that naturally draws people in and makes random selection feel exciting rather than mundane. It's a party tool, not an enterprise app.

### Emotional Goals
- **Excitement & suspense**: Build anticipation as the wheel spins and slows
- **Delight & surprise**: Make the reveal moment feel special and celebratory

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark theme with premium polish**: Keep the current dark foundation (#121212 bg, Spotify-inspired) but elevate with subtle gradients, glass/frosted effects, and refined micro-interactions
- **Accent color**: Green (#1DB954) as primary accent — vibrant and energetic
- **Typography**: Noto Sans SC with CJK fallbacks — clean, modern, weighted toward bold/semibold for headings
- **Wheel palette**: 20 vivid segment colors that pop against the dark background
- **Anti-references**: Avoid looking like a cheap casino or ad-heavy spinner site. No garish neon, no cluttered layouts, no gratuitous animation
- **Theme**: Dark mode only

### Design Principles
1. **Spectacle at the center** — The wheel is the hero. Everything else supports the spin experience. Layout, spacing, and visual weight should draw the eye to the wheel first.
2. **Premium playfulness** — Fun doesn't mean cheap. Use polished surfaces (glass, subtle gradients, refined shadows) to make vibrancy feel intentional and high-quality.
3. **Moment-driven motion** — Animate to build emotion: suspense during the spin, celebration on reveal, smooth feedback on interactions. Never animate for decoration alone.
4. **Instant clarity** — Labels, controls, and state changes should be immediately understood. Chinese UI copy should be concise. The app works for first-time users without explanation.
5. **Responsive and contained** — Two-column on desktop, single-column on mobile. The entire experience fits on screen without scrolling on desktop. Touch-friendly on all devices.

### Tech Stack
- Single `index.html` with inline CSS and vanilla JavaScript
- Canvas API for wheel rendering and animation
- Google Fonts (Noto Sans SC)
- CSS custom properties for theming
- No build tools, frameworks, or external dependencies
